Practice MCQ Questions

1. Choose the correct sentence:

  • A) She don't like coffee.
  • B) She doesn't like coffee.
  • C) She doesn't likes coffee.
  • D) She doesn't like coffees.
Correct Answer: B) She doesn't like coffee.
With third person singular (he, she, it), we use 'doesn't' (does not) and the base form of the verb 'like', not 'likes'.

2. Which word is a synonym for "happy"?

  • A) Sad
  • B) Angry
  • C) Joyful
  • D) Tired
Correct Answer: C) Joyful
A synonym is a word that has the same or similar meaning. 'Joyful' means the same as 'happy', expressing a feeling of pleasure or contentment.

3. What is the past tense of "go"?

  • A) went
  • B) goed
  • C) gone
  • D) going
Correct Answer: A) went
The past tense of the irregular verb 'go' is 'went'. 'Gone' is the past participle, used with 'have' or 'has'.

4. Choose the correct form: "I have _____ my homework."

  • A) do
  • B) did
  • C) done
  • D) doing
Correct Answer: C) done
With 'have' (present perfect tense), we use the past participle form. The past participle of 'do' is 'done'.

5. Which sentence uses the correct article?

  • A) I saw a elephant at the zoo.
  • B) I saw an elephant at the zoo.
  • C) I saw the elephant at a zoo.
  • D) I saw elephant at zoo.
Correct Answer: B) I saw an elephant at the zoo.
We use 'an' before words that start with a vowel sound (a, e, i, o, u). 'Elephant' starts with a vowel sound, so we use 'an'. 'The zoo' is correct when referring to a specific or known zoo.
